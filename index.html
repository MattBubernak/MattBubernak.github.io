About Me

During my five years at CU, internships at Qualcomm and Lockheed Martin, and hobbyist windows phone development, I've been exposed to a broad spectrum of software development. While these different areas have each taught me important lessons, the projects I've enjoyed most have been those with the widest scope; thus I've recently focused on full-stack web development. I'm drawn to the excitement of exploring the varying levels of the software stack, and the challenge of piecing it all together, and satisfaction presenting it with an effective user interface.


Some of my most memorable time at CU was spent as a Teaching Assistant(TA) for Data Structures and Principles of Programming Languages. Being a TA required teaching recitation sections, individually helping students, and grading. My experience as a TA influenced my choice to travel to Nepal in the Fall of 2016 to work as a computer teacher at the Surya Boarding School in Khandbari.


Balancing life with work is extremely important to me. I am an avid ultimate frisbee player, and played for the University of Colorado's club team, Mamabird, for my entire college career. Outside of ultimate, I enjoy rock climbing, camping, and hiking. It is my goal to hike all of Colorado's 14ers.
